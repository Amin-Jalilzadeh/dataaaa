In this database schema visualization, we provide an organized and detailed view of the data required for our project. The first column presents the various tables and clusters, with each table color-coded based on its level of importance. Clicking on a table reveals its attributes in the second column, while the third column provides further information related to data importance, source, usage phase, and more. Please note that this schema is a work in progress and will evolve as we conduct our literature review and establish our data infrastructure. This project is a part of a doctoral research effort, undertaken in collaboration with DATALESS. Thank you for your interest and support. Amin Jalilzadeh

Legend
Legend 1: Data Importance:
Essential Data: Data that is critical for the project and without which the project cannot proceed.
Valuable Data: Data that significantly enhances the project's quality or efficiency, but is not strictly necessary for the project to proceed.
Supplementary Data: Data that provides additional insights and may be helpful in specific cases, but is not essential to the overall project.
Optional Data: Data that could be included in the project if available, but does not have a significant impact on the project's success or quality.
Nice-to-Have Data: Data that could be interesting or useful to have, but is not a priority and does not have a direct impact on the project.
Additional Data: Data that may not be directly related to the project, but could provide some context or background information.
Legend 2: Data Source:
Open Data: Data that is publicly available and can be accessed without any restrictions.
Collected Data: Data that needs to be actively collected, for example through surveys, observations, or measurements.
Private Data: Data that is provided by or obtained from specific companies, organizations, or individuals and is not publicly available.
Government Data: Data that is provided by or obtained from government agencies or public institutions.
Purchased/Third-Party Data: Data that is obtained from third-party providers, such as data brokers, market research firms, or through purchase or licensing.
Generated Data: Data that is generated within your own organization or through simulations or models.
